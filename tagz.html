TagZ Foods:  A GenZ-Centric Snacking Brand

Anish Basu Roy and Sagar Bhalotia launched the snack food business TagZ Foods in 2019. The business is situated in Mumbai, India, and focuses on developing novel and nutritious snacks for Gen Z consumers. All of TagZ Foods' goods are vegan, gluten-free, and created using premium ingredients; none of them are fried. The company's snacks are likewise high in protein and fibre and low in calories and fat. A range of flavours, including Beer N Barbecue, Italian Cheese Dribblin', and Thai Vodka Tom Yumm, are offered in TagZ Foods' products. The business also provides a selection of gift baskets and snack packs that are ideal for corporate parties and special occasions.

Shark Tank Story


Shark Tank India, season 1, episode two featured TagZ Foods. Anish Basu Roy and Sagar Bhalotia, the company's founders, asked for an investment of 70 lakhs (US$90,000) in exchange for a 1% ownership stake. The goods from TagZ Foods, which use popped potato chips that are 50% less fatty than conventional fried chips, won over the Sharks. They were worried, though, about the founders' inexperience and the company's price. Ashneer Grover ultimately made an offer to the founders of 70 lakhs for 2.75% equity, valued at 25.45 crores (US$32 million).
TagZ Foods became the first business to receive funding on Shark Tank India when the founders accepted the offer. Sales for TagZ Foods have significantly increased as a result of their appearance on Shark Tank India. Additionally, the business has increased the number of flavours in its product lineup, along with the addition of popcorn and biscuits. Pre-Series A round investors 9 Unicorns, Dexter Angels, Agility Ventures, Venture Catalysts, Klub, Indifi, and Rannvijay Singha led the $2 million fundraising round for TagZ Foods in 2023. The money will be used by the business to expand into new markets, speed up growth, and introduce new goods.
